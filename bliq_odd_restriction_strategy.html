Aggressive ODD Restriction across geography · context · operational support

The core small-team move: deliberately shrink the operating domain so the validation problem becomes tractable, certifiable, and affordable — then relax one constraint at a time as data and capital grow.

1
Geography
  • One Berlin district
  • Standard / pre-validated routes only
  • Hard geofence
  • Breach → Minimal-Risk Maneuver
Why: Map once, reuse. Validation effort scales with area — keep it to ~20–30 km of road, not 500+.
ISO 22737 (LSAD) — ODD definition for fixed routesAFGBV — defined operating-area approval
2
Speed ≤ 50 km/h
  • Hard limit, enforced
  • Compute + vehicle-interface watchdog
  • Applies across whole district
Why: Pedestrian outcomes survivable; wider latency tolerance; prediction more tractable with more reaction time.
Mobileye RSS — safe-distance / reaction-time envelopeISO 22737 — low-speed (≤32 km/h) AD precedent
3
Weather + Daylight
  • Dry → light rain only
  • Daylight only
  • No fog / heavy rain / snow / night
  • Continuous weather monitor
Why: Weather drives perception failures — defer weather-robust ML to year 2–3 when the data cost is justified.
ISO 21448 (SOTIF) — performance limits of perceptionMobileye CES (Amnon) — perception under degraded conditions

4
Time Window
  • Weekday off-peak
  • e.g. Tue–Thu, 10:00–16:00
  • Avoid rush hours + weekends
Why: Predictable traffic shrinks the long tail — fewer pedestrians, stabler interactions, fewer scenarios to validate.
Waymo — long-tail / ODD scoping (Anguelov)ISO 21448 (SOTIF) — scenario coverage argument
5
Remote Supervision
  • 1 : N supervisor (N ≤ 3–5)
  • Advisory only — not teleop steering
  • Link loss → safe stop
  • Permitted under AFGBV §1f StVG
Why: A second safety barrier that is cheaper to scale than full hardware redundancy.
AFGBV §1f — technical-supervisor conceptISO 21434 — secure remote connectivity

Why it wins on budget

  • Collapses the validation space — fewer scenarios, less simulation, smaller test fleet.
  • Defers the expensive problems (weather, night, high speed, unrestricted routing) to later, funded phases.
  • Each constraint can be relaxed one at a time as data and capital grow — staged, measurable expansion.
  • Turns Bliq's resource limits into a defensible, certifiable scope instead of a disadvantage.
