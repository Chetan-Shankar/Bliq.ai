Hybrid-C = ML perception (learned BEV front-ends, Approach A) feeding a safety-constrained classical planner (certifiable interfaces, Approach B). Deployed by every commercial L4 operator today (Waymo, Cruise, Zoox, Mobileye).
Aurix TC4x replaces the "satellite ECU" concept from production ADAS (e.g. MB L2++ architecture). In L3 Robotaxi there is no human fallback ODD — the redundancy must be deterministic ASIL-D, not a reduced-ODD compute node.
Safety Gate implements RSS (Responsibility-Sensitive Safety) reachability analysis. Any trajectory from the ML planner that violates the safety envelope is rejected and re-planned or escalated to MRM. This is the certifiability boundary under ISO 26262 / SOTIF.
Remote Ops (ROPS) supports the L3 driverless use case: a human operator monitors a fleet, assists edge-case resolution, and can trigger MRM remotely. Commands are always routed through Aurix to preserve independence from the main compute path and from 5G link integrity.
