★ Stated assumption (drives every number below):
  • Single Berlin district, ~10 km radius (~20–30 km of validated road), NOT a city-wide rollout.
  • Peak fleet ~15–20 retrofit vehicles — a tight-ODD service fleet, not a 300–500 commercial fleet.
  • ODD: ≤50 km/h, daylight, dry–light rain, weekday off-peak; remote supervisor under AFGBV §1f.
  • Hybrid stack on Autoware + safety MCU; foundation models offline only.
  • TÜV / KBA process runs roughly to plan (a generous assumption — see risks).
This is deliberately not a 300–500-vehicle commercial fleet. At a ~10 km single-district ODD with daylight/dry/off-peak limits, a peak fleet of ~15–20 retrofit vehicles is the honest number — which lowers the fleet, insurance and ops lines accordingly versus a city-wide plan.

Assumptions · Risks · Bottlenecks

Biggest risks

  • Regulatory timeline is the dominant risk — AFGBV operating-area approval has little precedent; a 6–12 month slip is plausible and outside engineering control.
  • The long tail of urban edge cases — even a tight ODD contains jaywalkers, construction, emergency vehicles, ambiguous junctions; the last 5% of disengagements costs more than the first 95%.
  • Remote-supervision economics — if the sustainable supervisor ratio stays near 1:3–1:4, the service stays loss-making; the whole model depends on improving it.
  • Capital-market risk — the plan needs a Series B and C; an AV-funding winter (post-Cruise) could strand the company mid-build.
  • Connectivity-loss failure mode — unique to the remote-supervision model; must degrade to a safe stop, and that path itself needs certifying.

Most likely bottlenecks

  • Validation throughput, not model accuracy, is the rate-limiter. Turning supervisor interventions into validated releases is the slowest loop.
  • TÜV/KBA assessment bandwidth — a small number of qualified assessors gate every safety-case milestone.
  • Senior generalist hiring in years 1–2 — people who can span vehicle + autonomy + safety are scarce in Berlin and set the pace of everything else.
  • Simulation-to-road correlation — the safety case ultimately needs real miles no amount of sim can substitute.
